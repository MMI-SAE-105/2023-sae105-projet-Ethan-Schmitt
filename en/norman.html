‘Norman’ by Steven Price: A Symphony of Courage

"Norman" is a song from the soundtrack of the film "Fury", composed by Steven Price. It is played at the end of the film during a wide top-down shot of the M4A3E8 Sherman. This music evokes the character of Norman Ellison, played by Logan Lerman, who is the new gunner of the "Fury" tank.

Composition:

The musical composition, with a duration of 2 minutes and 50 seconds, incorporates violins, piano, vocals, and percussion. It starts softly with a few piano notes accompanied by vocals. Gradually, the violins and percussion join the other instruments, creating a harmonious and pleasant sound.

Interpretation:

The song "Norman" is a true symphony of courage that perfectly reflects the character of Norman Ellison in the film. The initial gentleness of the piano notes and vocals represents Norman's innocence and naivety when he is first introduced as the new gunner of the "Fury" tank. As the melody develops, the gradual introduction of violins and percussion symbolizes the challenges faced by Norman, as well as his transformation into a fearless soldier. The harmony created by the orchestration reflects the camaraderie and teamwork that characterize the "Fury" tank crew. This musical composition perfectly illustrates Norman's emotional journey and the group dynamics within the crew.

Impact:

Music plays a crucial role in enhancing the emotional impact of the film. In particular, the song "Norman" helps highlight the bravery and sacrifice of Norman and his crew, while providing viewers with an immersive cinematic experience.

In conclusion, "Norman" is more than just a musical composition. It is a symphony of courage that tells a story of transformation, courage, and sacrifice. It is the perfect reflection of the emotional journey that the character of Norman Ellison goes through in the film Fury.
