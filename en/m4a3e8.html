The American M4A3E8 Sherman Tank

Ethan Schmitt

08/01/2024

The M4A3E8 Sherman, also known as the Easy Eight, is one of the most iconic tanks of World War II. It was produced in large quantities, with nearly fifty thousand units (all versions combined).

Technical Specifications:

Type: Medium Tank
Crew: 5 (commander, gunner, loader, driver, assistant driver/bow machine gunner)
Length: 5.89 m
Width: 2.62 m
Height: 2.74 m
Combat Weight: 27.2 t
Main Armament: One 75 mm M3 gun
Secondary Armament: One 7.62 mm Browning machine gun in the bow; one coaxial 7.62 mm Browning machine gun; one optional 12.7 mm Browning M2 machine gun on the turret
Engine: Ford GAA V8 gasoline; 450 hp (336 kW) at 2,600 rpm
Top Speed on Road: 34 km/h
Fuel Capacity: 662 l
Range: 193 km

Battle Performance

The Sherman M4A3E8 was widely used by the Allies during World War II. It was deployed in almost all theaters of war, including most of North Africa, Russia, most of Europe, the East Indies, the Philippines, numerous Pacific islands, and China. Despite its massive production and widespread use, the Sherman had its flaws. It was inferior to German tanks like the Tiger or Panther in terms of armor and firepower. However, it compensated for these shortcomings with its reliability, ease of maintenance, and mass production.
