Fury: A Brutal Immersion into Tank Battles

Ethan Schmitt

08/01/2024

We are in April 1945 in Germany, aboard the M4A3E8 Sherman tank named Fury, commanded by Sergeant Don Wardaddy. His crew consists of four men: Boyd Swan, Trini Garcia, Grady Travis, and the new recruit, Norman Ellison, who replaces Red, who fell in combat. Together, their mission is to assist Captain Waggoner. He will ask them to clear the way to the next town. A mission that will prove to be highly perilous...

Released on October 22, 2014, and directed by David Ayer, Fury is a war film set in the final weeks of World War II. Starring actors like Brad Pitt, Shia LaBeouf, and Jon Bernthal.

Characters:

Sergeant Don 'Wardaddy' Collier (Brad Pitt): He is the commander of the M4A3E8 Sherman tank. He is a courageous and determined leader, who can sometimes be ruthless, as seen with Norman at the beginning of the film. However, he has a good heart, as revealed towards the end of the film, as his main concern is keeping his men alive.

Boyd 'Bible' Swan (Shia LaBeouf): He is the tank's gunner. His character is deeply religious and is known for his great precision and dedication, especially in the battle against the German Tiger tank. However, he is also quite emotional and attentive to others.

Corporal Trini 'Gordo' Garcia (Michael Peña): He is the tank's driver. His character is skilled, and his ability to navigate difficult terrains is crucial for the success of the mission. He is of Mexican origin, which explains why he sometimes speaks Spanish. He is a character with a good heart and is very effective in combat, especially in the maneuvers performed during the battle against the German Tiger tank.

Grady 'Coon-Ass' Travis (Jon Bernthal): He is the tank's loader-mechanic. His character is tough and reliable, and his knowledge of the tank has been more than useful in various repairs to get the tank back in working order, especially at the beginning of the film when he fixes the engine. He is a rather violent, perverse, and tough character, especially towards Norman, but he will eventually become grateful and admiring towards this young recruit.

Norman Ellison 'Machine' (Logan Lerman): He is the new co-pilot-gunner who replaces Red, who fell in combat. Despite his inexperience, he must quickly adapt to the brutal reality of war. He is a fragile character at the beginning of the film but becomes increasingly courageous over time. He shows exceptional bravery by being willing to sacrifice himself alongside Sergeant Wardaddy to slow down the enemy.

Review

- "Fury" received generally positive reviews. On AlloCiné, the film has an average rating of 4.1 out of 5 based on 22829 ratings. One critic mentioned that "Fury" produces an impressive effect and even shock. On SensCritique, the film has a rating of 6.7 out of 10. The reviews are mixed, with some praising the realism and intensity of the film.

- However, "Fury" also received negative reviews. Some critics pointed out pacing issues that hinder the smooth flow of the film. EcranLarge criticized the character of Logan Lerman, calling him a completely unnecessary addition.

In summary, the film offers an intense and realistic portrayal of tank battles during World War II. Despite some negative reviews, the film was generally well-received for its realism and the performances of its actors. It remains a poignant testament to the horrors of war and the courage of the men who fought in it.
